FUE — Extraction vs Excision

A significant modification has been made in the world of hair restoration surgery procedures. The popular FUE hair restoration procedure which is previously known as follicular unit extraction (FUE) is now to be known as a follicular unit excision. As of 2018, The International Society of Hair Restoration Surgery (ISHRS), Board of Governors has changed the name of hair transplantation technique known as FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction ) to FUE (Follicular Unit Excision ) to clarify the surgical aspects of the procedure. “The purpose of clarifying that surgical graft removal with FUE involves excisions of hair and tissue is to make this terminology more scientifically, clinically and surgically accurate,” said Ricardo Mejia, MD, ISHRS board member and chair of the ad hoc committee on issues pertaining to the unlicensed practice of medicine. “When performed by qualified, properly trained hair restoration surgeons, hair transplantation is a safe and highly effective procedure to correct hair loss. However, it is a cosmetic surgical procedure and any attempt to downplay the significant surgical aspects of FUE to appear less invasive is misleading and potentially dangerous to patients.” Dr. Mejia explained that the term “extraction” implies to some patients a nonsurgical procedure that only involves “extracting” hairs as if they were being plucked out of the scalp without surgery. “Amending FUE terminology is one more way that we can inform patients about the true surgical nature of the procedure so they can make better decisions about their choice of physician and treatment,” he said.

Why change Extraction to Excision?

.Changing well-known medical terminology isn't taken gently . Asking patients and experts alike to embrace new language are often sophisticated and confusing. In spite of performing the FUE procedure for so many years, specialists started to see basic issues with the term extraction. Many believed that it made the technique sound less intrusive than it really was, the patient thinks that the hair was just plucked out of the scalp instead of by thousands of little incisions made throughout the procedure. By joining these two basic steps, the term extraction was conceived: Incision + Extraction = Excision
